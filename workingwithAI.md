# Working with AI

## 1. Working with AI

Go-To-Market Strategy: AI Legal Document Review Tool for Mid-Size Law Firms

1. Ideal Customer Profile

- Target mid-size law firms (20–200 lawyers) handling:
  - Corporate law
  - Litigation / discovery
  - Real estate contracts
  - Employment law
  - M&A due diligence

- Pain points:
  - Billable hours lost to repetitive review
  - Slow turnaround times
  - Junior associate overload
  - Human error risk
  - Pressure to modernize

2. Positioning

- “Cut document review time by 70% while improving accuracy and protecting privilege.”
- Position as AI assistant for lawyers, not replacement.

3. Product Offer

- Core features:
  - Contract clause extraction
  - Risk flagging
  - Redline suggestions
  - Summaries
  - Privilege/confidentiality detection
  - Bulk review workflow
  - Audit trail & citations

- Must-have:
  - SOC 2 / strong security
  - Private cloud / on-prem options
  - Human-in-the-loop controls

4. Pricing Strategy

- For mid-size firms:
  - Per seat monthly pricing
  - Usage-based document volume tier
  - Annual contracts with discount
  - Free pilot for 30 days

- Example:
  - $299/user/month basic
  - $799/team/month pro
  - Enterprise custom

5. Acquisition Channels

- Direct Sales (Primary)
  - Outbound to managing partners, innovation heads, practice leads
  - LinkedIn outreach + email
- Content Marketing
  - Guides: “How firms cut review time with AI”
  - Webinars on AI compliance for firms
  - Case studies
- Partnerships
  - Legal tech consultants
  - Bar associations
  - Practice management software vendors
- Events
  - Legal conferences
  - Regional bar meetings
  - CLE educational sessions

6. Sales Process

- Demo
- ROI calculator
- 2-week pilot using their real docs
- Security review
- Paid annual contract
- Expand to more practice groups

7. Objection Handling

- AI hallucinations? Human review required + citations
- Confidentiality? Encrypted/private deployment
- Lawyers resist change? Saves time, doesn’t replace judgment
- Cost? Cheaper than manual review hours

8. Expansion Strategy

- Land one team → expand to:
  - Litigation
  - Corporate
  - Employment
  - Real estate
  - Whole firm license

9. KPIs

- Cost per qualified lead
- Demo-to-pilot conversion
- Pilot-to-paid conversion
- Average contract value
- Time saved per client
- Expansion revenue

10. Sharp Advice

- Do not sell “AI.” Sell:
  - Faster client turnaround
  - More profitable matters
  - Less associate burnout
  - Better margins
  - Competitive advantage

- Law firms buy outcomes, not technology.

## 2. Working without AI

This is my Go to Market without AI:

1) Target Customers:
Mid-size law firms

2) Value Proposition:
There are trillions of legal documents. This tool would erase the manual review process and make it fast and automated, saving money and time.

3) Pricing:
Usage-based.

4) Channels:
Ads on social and in-house enterprise sales, which the CEO should do at the start and then the VP of Sales.

5) Messaging:
Review millions of documents in a few minutes and deliver value to customers fast and cheap.

6) Launch + Growth Plan:
Launch to beta users (which are some law firms), improve the product with their feedback, and then sell by going to enterprises one-by-one. Sales at scale would be achieved by hiring a lot of salespeople for cold calling or direct outreach.
